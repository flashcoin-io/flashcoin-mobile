FLASH Privacy Policy

Flashnet Tech Inc. (“Flashnet” or “us”), amongst other things, publishes digital representations of virtual coins that have no value (“FLASH”).

Protecting the privacy of users of this web site (the “Site”) is important to us. This privacy policy (the “Privacy Policy”) sets out the general terms by which the Flashnet collects, stores and discloses personal information on this Site. From time to time, Flashnet may modify this Privacy Policy without prior notice to you.

  1. You are Free to Supply or Not Supply Personal Information

    The only information we collect about you on this Site is information that you voluntarily provide to Flashnet. This information may include, for example, name, mailing address, Email address, phone number and similar information. If you supply us with personal information on this Site, we will use that information only to contact you so that we can:

    • 1.1. Market our products and services and those of third parties to you;
    • 1.2. Respond to your request for information or documentation;
    • 1.3. Combat fraud, money laundering and the funding of terrorism; and
    • 1.4. Occasionally send you other information about Flashnet and its services that we believe you would find interesting.

  2. Browsing Only

    Like most standard web site servers we use log files. This includes internet protocol (IP) addresses, browser type, internet service provider (ISP), referring/exit pages, platform type, date/time stamp, and number of clicks to analyze trends, administer the site, track user's movement in the aggregate, and gather broad demographic information for aggregate use. IP addresses, etc. are not linked to personally identifiable information unless you instruct us to complete an application for services from one of our Suppliers in which case we will log your IP address together with other information submitted on such an application form. This Site may use cookies or web beacons solely to aggregate information about the pages users visit so we can update and redesign, as necessary, our Site.

    From time to time, however, we may disclose this information to fulfill legal or regulatory obligations or if we determine such disclosure is legally advisable or necessary to protect our rights and the rights of others, or to prevent harm. In addition, we may aggregate data about users of our site and use it for statistical purposes.

  3. Privacy and Security are Flashnet Priorities

    We take every precaution to protect our users' information. When users submit sensitive information via the Site, their information is protected both online and off-line. Only employees or consultants, if any, who need the information to perform a specific job are granted access to personally identifiable information. Our employees and consultants, if any, must use password-protected screen-savers when they leave their Flashnet portal. When they return, they must re-enter their password to re-gain access to user information. Furthermore, all employees and consultants, if any, are kept up-to-date on our security and privacy practices. As well, any time new policies are added, our employees and consultants, if any, are notified and/or reminded about the importance we place on privacy, and what they can do to ensure our users' information is protected. Finally, the servers that store personally identifiable information are in a secure environment, in a locked facility. If you have any questions about the security at our Site, users can send an email to Site at the address provided at the contact section of this site.

    Flashnet may disclose personal information collected online to third parties as well as our affiliates who assist us with obtaining or supplying services to you that you have requested. In some cases, this may require your information to be sent to other countries. By supplying your information on this Site, you consent to your information being transferred across national borders to Flashnet and third parties.
